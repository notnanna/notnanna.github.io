Q1)What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?
Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions


When interacting with this experience, the first thing I noticed was the little key pad with the symbols - after noticing that, my eyes were led to the little prompter above it, which began instructing me on how to play the game. At first I was confused, I thought that one I clicked the requested symbol it would lead me to a sort of home screen where I would be able to do several different things, but after several different tries, I had realised that the screen was trying to get me to play a memory game which I had failed at an embarrassing amount of times.


After having a little play with the memory game, I moved around the screen, I began just pressing random buttons and sections of the screen - some of which didn’t really have much of a purpose but to look pretty.


While navigating through the site I found that the main part of it revolved around the memory game, but after pressing the ‘i’ icon I was then redirected to another page that went on to explain the background and a brief description of the “world of downstream”.


 My discrete actions:


  • I began with the little symbol keypad - randomly pressing buttons
  • I then played the memory game after realising its purpose
  • After I played with the IQ adjuster on the left of the screen , and then played the symbol game again to see if much had changed
  • I went on to press different icons to see if they did much ~ the smiley face, ‘site by wondermaker’, volume icon, current playtest/inactive icons, the hexwood frame, play test report, ‘i’ icon

And then after having a little play with the little icons, I had noticed that there was a power button in the top left corner, so naturally I pressed it. Then the screen went dark, so I tried pressing the button again and then all the lights lit up and the screen returned to its normal state


Q2)What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with? What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?


I spent most of my time interacting with the symbol keypad and the memory game. I think that might’ve been because I initially didn’t understand that it was a game and that the keypad was connected to the dialogue screen above. So my competitive streak came in, and I decided that I wanted to beat the game. I then ended up spending a good 5-10 minutes trying to beat the game, and surprisingly enough, my goldfish memory didn’t disappoint me (too much). I ended up bearing the game after two or so attempts, and then when I wanted to try my luck one last time my memory failed me.


Q3) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?


At first I had trouble trying to figure out the purpose of this site, but after reading the discovery page, I pieced together the idea that the intended primary goal of the interactive experience was to gather intel through their open code site to apply it to ‘Downstream’, the game they were still in the process of developing.


After finding this out, I thought that it was an interesting approach to building their game. As they mentioned in their explanation, many gaming companies don’t really show audiences their early developing phase (not at the start at least), but I think that this direction allows them to get feedback and input quite early, while also getting active information as they are in the process of development which could prove to have a strong impact in the results of the final product of their project.


Q4)How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?


I personally don’t think the initial experience communicated the objective very well, as I was confused at its purpose at first, and only found it out after touching many buttons that led me to another page with further explanation. The main page looks like a sort of game page, so it was difficult to understand its purpose beyond that, and once I figured out that the main thing I could interact with was the memory game I became even more confused.


But I do find that having a separate page for the extended information on the objective of this project was a smart decision as it allowed me to have two focus on one portion bit by bit and to not get too overstimulated by an excessive amount of content.


Q5)What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)


I think that the site is fun, so maybe it should be interacted with a handful of times here and there, but as I’ve only really interacted with it a handful of times myself I wouldn’t be able to make an accurate guess.


But since the whole idea of this interactive site is to collect intel and information for the advancement of their game, I think the duration of the interactivity really depends on the timeframe of their project. Even so, I think that it would be great to have the site up past the time the game has been fully developed as it could help them understand their audience, have specific information to fall back on, especially if they are looking to further advance the game, make updates or even create a new game. So I believe that a healthy amount of people interacting with the site, frequently (no specific amount) would prove to be great for the developers of the game!


Q6)How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?


Once again, I don’t believe that the team behind this site effectively and clearly communicated the purpose of their interactive experience, as it may take time for someone to piece together what could be their objective through the brief information they provided. But from what I’ve been able to gather, I believe that it communicates that the site should be interacted with frequently and over different durations of time, as it’ll take a while to gather intel and also develop the game. And observing how people interact with the site itself is also a crucial factor in collecting accurate data.


 Week 3


Q1)What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?


When first introduced to this to this site, the first sort of media it reminded me of was the game ‘Jetpack joyride’ and the power plant in ‘The Simpsons’ - I completely understand that the site may have not drawn any inspiration from the game nor the tv series at all, but I have noticed a very clear connection in terms of the both the game and setting’s visual design, each of which have a very old school and industrial sort of design.


Also, when looking at the little simulation of the game that they had in the bottom right corner l, it’s design also reminded me of the game clash of clans - I could see the similarities in how the game’s layout was, connecting it to the the animation design, the outdoor setting, connections to the idea of nature and finally the idea that the game revolves around a “post-singularity civilisation simulator and extendable multiplayer”, where the environment would be grown and cultivated by the player.


Q2)What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?


These references communicate to me that you can find inspiration anywhere, but also that you can take bits and pieces from different things to help create your final piece, but make them your own without copying. I have also found through this site that considering your own objective and audience and its relation to your project can be incredibly important when looking for source material to reference or gain inspiration from.


Q3)What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?


The most frustrating part of the interaction for me was that most of what I could do on the main page revolved around a small and simple game, and that a lot of the icons on the page had not been fully thought out. I personally wanted more out of the site in terms of deeper interactions, but felt really bored and disappointed when I came to the realisation that a lot of the buttons were kind of for show (meaning that they would make little sounds, turn lights on and off but not do much beyond that fact).


Q4)What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?


Unfortunately, I didn’t really find too much of the interaction to be exciting, let alone satisfying, but I will say that I did enjoy the visual design. As I mentioned previously, the design reminded me of the game ‘Jetpack Joyride’, which I played a lot when I was younger, so it gave me a sense of nostalgia. That being said, the expectations I had once finding the similarities in appearances may have led me to overthink this interaction, leading me to disappointment.
